Master Thesis: P3C-LiB: Partial Constant Current Charging Profile Approach for Estimating Lithium-Ion Battery Capacity Using Deep Learning Techniques
Developed the noval P3C-LiB Deep Learning model in collaboration with BMW AG and DFKI GmbH, based on the much underestimated data from the Constant Current Charging phase to predict the capacity of lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles. The model demonstrated state-of-the-art performance, achieving a mean absolute percentage error of 0.49% in the best-case scenario and 1.27% in the worst-case setup. Its lightweight architecture ensures compatibility with the Battery Management Systems of EVs, offering an efficient and reliable solution for practical implementation.
Advisors: Prof. Philipp Slusallek, Dr. Christian Müller, Igor Vozniak, Dr. Thinh Nguyen-Xuan, and Li Jiahao.
